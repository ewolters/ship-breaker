# Unit Economics Analysis

**Date:** 2026-05-02
**Status:** Preliminary — order-of-magnitude estimates for feasibility assessment

---

## The Structural Disadvantage

Bangladesh yards *receive* $475-490/LDT from the shipowner (the owner gets paid to dispose). US yards *charge* the owner roughly $50-150/LDT. That's a **$500-650/LDT swing per ton** — for a 10,000 LDT VLCC, a ~$6M structural disadvantage built entirely from labor, hazmat externalization, and infrastructure cost.

## Where the Gap Actually Lives

Most people assume it's labor. It isn't, fully:

| Cost Category | Bangladesh | US Automated | Gap per Ship |
|---|---|---|---|
| Per-ship labor | ~$20-50K | ~$200-400K | ~$200-400K |
| Hazmat treatment (Bangladesh dumps) | ~$0 | ~$300-500K | ~$300-500K |
| Capital amortization (dock + automation) | Minimal | ~$300-500K | ~$300-500K |
| Insurance + compliance | Minimal | ~$100-200K | ~$100-200K |
| **Legitimate cost difference** | | | **~$1-1.5M/ship** |

The remaining $4-5M of the $6M gap is **pure rent extraction by regulatory arbitrage** — Bangladesh's price reflects the value of getting to skip everything Western jurisdictions would require.

## How a US Automated Operation Gets to Viable

Not by competing on commodity scrap. The economics only work if **hazmat-as-product** is real:

### Revenue Stream 1: Hazmat Treatment as Revenue Center
- PCBs at $1,000-3,000/tonne destruction fees
- Asbestos abatement at $400-800/tonne
- Contaminated marine oils at $200-500/tonne
- Typical end-of-life vessel: 50-200 tonnes treatable hazmat
- **Per-ship revenue: $50-600K**
- Critical: the treatment line takes **external feedstock**, so it's not throughput-limited by ship arrivals

### Revenue Stream 2: ESG-Premium Dismantlement
- Maersk, MSC, NYK, and the green shipping cohort already pay above-market for IHM compliance under the EU Ship Recycling Regulation
- Certified clean-dismantle premium of $100-200/LDT plausible for owners with public sustainability commitments
- **Per-ship premium: $1-2M** on a 10K LDT vessel

### Revenue Stream 3: Navy + MARAD Captive Market
- US government ships must be dismantled domestically
- ~$50-100M/year in contracts, currently going to a small number of fragmented yards
- Automated bidder with better safety stats and lower per-ship dwell wins on procurement criteria, not just price

### Revenue Stream 4: High-Grade Material Recovery
- Properly separated steel (decontaminated, sorted by alloy) sells into auto/aerospace recyclers at **$50-150/tonne premiums** vs. construction rebar
- Bangladesh can't do this because their cutting and melting destroys the grade signal
- Horizontal laminar slicing preserves alloy separation by processing one layer at a time

### Revenue Stream 5: Federal Cost-Sharing
- IRA funds, EPA brownfield/Superfund tie-ins, port infrastructure grants
- Mesocosm + automation buildout is plausibly **30-50% federally cost-shared**
- Reduces effective capex from $50-80M to $25-55M

## Labor Productivity

| Metric | Bangladesh | US Automated | Improvement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Workers per yard | 300-600 | 50-100 | 5-10x |
| Ships per year | 5-15 | ~10 | Comparable |
| Worker-years per ship | 30-90 | 5-20 | 5-10x |

## The Variable That Swings the Answer

Whether hazmat treatment can stand on its own as a **profit center independent of ship arrivals.**

- **If yes:** You're an industrial recycler whose feedstock acquisition mechanism happens to be ship dismantlement. The unit economics change qualitatively. Ship gate fees become one input among many.
- **If no:** You're a subsidized boutique operation that survives on Navy contracts and ESG premiums — viable but not transformational.

## Summary

| Parameter | Estimate |
|---|---|
| Capex | $50-80M ($25-55M after federal cost-share) |
| Throughput | ~10 ships/year |
| Headcount | ~80 |
| Breakeven | Year 4-6 |
| Hub-and-spoke | Non-negotiable for the model |

The hub-and-spoke architecture from the morphological analysis is the load-bearing insight: the hub (hazmat treatment + materials recovery) is the business. The spokes (demolition berths) are the intake mechanism.
