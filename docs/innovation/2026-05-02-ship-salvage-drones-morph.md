## Random Combinations Evaluated

### Combination 1
Envelope cert / Hydraulic gradient / Calibration drone pair / Guild-succession / Cold-stabilization / Modular 90-day

**Feasible:** With modification. Hydraulic gradient requires site-specific hydrogeological survey incompatible with 90-day deployment timeline. Modification: pre-qualify sites geologically before kit deployment.

**Interesting:** The calibration drone pair suggests a *differential epistemology* — you don't need absolute state, only relative deviation from a paired reference. Reframes sensor confidence from "how accurate is the sensor" to "how similar are two identically-treated measurements."

### Combination 2
Envelope cert / Pontoon skirt / Reactive coating / Horizontal laminar slicing / Pharma fractionation / Permanent single-berth

**Feasible:** Yes. Most internally consistent combination in the set.

**Interesting:** Horizontal laminar slicing accidentally solves the blueprint-reality gap not by measuring unknown state but by *making it irrelevant* — you encounter the ship one horizontal slice at a time, and whatever is there gets processed. Reactive coatings work best when you know what surface chemistry to expect, and laminar slicing provides exactly that predictability. Pontoon skirt fits naturally because laminar slicing produces predictable debris fall patterns. Orthogonal containment — skirt catches lateral contamination, laminar geometry constrains vertical.

### Combination 3
Envelope cert / Hydraulic gradient / Pulse-and-dwell / Guild-succession / Subterranean vault / Shared multi-tenant

**Feasible:** No. Multi-tenant + century-scale vault creates a permanent liability commons problem — which tenant owns the contamination? Modification: restrict vault to single facility owner via long-term operating license.

**Interesting:** Guild-succession paired with pulse-and-dwell is genuinely novel. Guild-succession treats demolition crew as ecological guilds — specialists who enter a zone, complete it, and hand off — which maps well onto the pulse-and-dwell rhythm. This sub-combination deserves extraction regardless.

### Combination 4
Envelope cert / Pontoon skirt / Pulse-and-dwell / Horizontal laminar / Pharma fractionation / Modular 90-day

**Feasible:** With modification. Pharma-grade fractionation incompatible with 90-day modular deployment — cannot amortize separation infrastructure. Modification: fractionation at permanent regional hub; modular kit handles demolition only.

**Interesting:** Pontoon skirt + laminar slicing creates layered containment logic — skirt catches lateral, laminar constrains vertical. Two independent containment mechanisms on orthogonal axes. Emergent redundancy, not designed-in redundancy.

### Combination 5
Actuarial authorization / Pontoon skirt / Calibration drone pair / Scored-performance cue stack / Cold-stabilization / Hub-and-spoke

**Feasible:** Yes. Most commercially legible combination.

**Interesting:** Insurance epistemology enforces information architecture coherence. Actuarial authorization means an insurer prices the operation, forcing explicit risk quantification. This demands scored performance (auditable triggers), which demands calibration drone pairs (quantified confidence intervals). The authorization mechanism selects for the sensor and sequencing approach downstream. A path to private capital without public health emergency declaration.

### Combination 6
Public health emergency / Sheet pile + grout / Pulse-and-dwell / Guild-succession / In-situ bioremediation / Permanent single-berth

**Feasible:** Yes. Most conceptually coherent with the public health reframing thesis.

**Interesting:** Bioremediation is usually considered slow and uncertain. But public health emergency declaration provides exactly the extended site-control tenure that slow bioremediation needs. The urgency of the declaration and the patience of the remediation are complements, not contradictions. Imports the entire regulatory and technical vocabulary of Superfund-style contaminated site remediation into ship breaking.

### Combination 7
Envelope cert / Constructed wetland / Reactive coating / Progressive flooding / In-situ bioremediation / Multi-tenant

**Feasible:** No. Progressive flooding introduces uncontrolled fluid movement that defeats wetland biofilm integrity. Two dimensions actively undermine each other.

**Interesting:** Demonstrates a hard incompatibility: progressive flooding cannot coexist with biological containment. Fluid dynamics override biological design.

### Combination 8
Public health emergency / Hydraulic gradient / Historical record fusion / Horizontal laminar / Thermal destruction + heat recovery / Hub-and-spoke

**Feasible:** Yes. Historical record fusion is the most intellectually honest response to the blueprint gap — uses fleet-wide priors to bound the unknown.

**Interesting:** Hub-and-spoke + thermal destruction inverts the conventional facility model — the hub is a *waste-to-energy facility*, and the spokes are modular demolition sites feeding it. The hazmat fate infrastructure is the center, not the berth. This reframes the business as an energy/materials company that happens to dismantle ships.

## Unexpected Dimension Interactions

**Authorization selects sensor and sequencing downstream.** The authorization mechanism isn't just a governance wrapper — it imposes an information epistemology that constrains viable sensor and sequencing options. Actuarial authorization demands scored performance and quantified confidence. Public health declaration demands epidemiological reporting and permits slow treatment. Envelope certification permits adaptive logic. This dependency was invisible before evaluating full combinations.

**Laminar slicing is a blueprint-gap workaround, not a sequencing choice.** It reframes Challenge 4 (the gap between blueprint and reality is a categorical unknown) from an information problem to a geometry problem. No other sequencing option does this. This makes it the only option that doesn't require solving the sensor fidelity problem first.

**Bioremediation needs slow authorization, not fast authorization.** Public health emergency declaration is intuitively "urgent," but it provides exactly the extended site-control tenure that bioremediation requires. Urgency and patience are complements.

**Multi-tenant is structurally incompatible with century-scale hazmat fate.** Any subterranean vault or long-horizon bioremediation paired with multi-tenant creates an unowned liability. This governance constraint functions like a hard engineering constraint.

**Hub-and-spoke inverts what the "facility" is.** When thermal destruction or pharma fractionation is at the hub, the business is a materials/energy company that happens to dismantle ships. This is a fundamentally different pitch to investors than a demolition company with a waste problem.
