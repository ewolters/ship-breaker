## Domain Solutions

### Cuisine / Restaurant Operations

**Reframe:** The ship is a whole-animal carcass that must be broken down. Currently this gets pushed to the cheapest hands — a prep cook in a cramped corner with bad lighting, no drainage, and a dull knife. Fat, blood, and offal run everywhere.

**Solution:** Build a dedicated breakdown station — a single-carcass butchery cell with automated overhead rail systems (for suspending and rotating the carcass), a tiered drainage floor pitched at 3 degrees toward subterranean collection channels (fat captured separately from blood, no cross-contamination), and the CLASP system functioning as the **expeditor** from the French brigade — a single coordinator who calls every action, sequences every hand, and prevents two people from reaching for the same joint at the same time. The expeditor does not cook. The expeditor only orchestrates.

Vertical segment support maps to a **carcass hook and rail system** — you do not hold a lamb shoulder with your hands while you separate it. You hook it, let gravity do the work, and use both hands for the knife. Recon first (visual inspection, identifying fault lines), then primary breakdown (primals separated), then secondary (sub-primals portioned), then cold storage (each portion logged and routed before the next cut begins). No portion touches the floor. No cut begins before its receiving container is confirmed empty and ready.

**Structural Principle:** *Isolate the messy transformation into a purpose-built cell, remove humans from the hazardous contact zone through tooling and suspension, capture byproducts at the point of generation before they can migrate, and coordinate all parallel actions through a single sequencing intelligence that prevents collision.*

### Epidemiology / Contagion Dynamics

**Reframe:** The ship is a hyperendemic disease reservoir carrying a dense, heterogeneous pathogen load — heavy metals, persistent organic pollutants, and hydrocarbons functioning as slow-release infectious agents. Current practice deploys unprotected community health workers directly into the hot zone. This is not a random outbreak — it is a predictable, engineered epidemic we are choosing to sustain.

**Solution:** Three classical epidemiological tools:

1. **Sentinel Surveillance Network** — Recon drones as autonomous sentinel nodes performing real-time syndromic surveillance before a case becomes an outbreak. They characterize the pathogen geography of the host before any invasive procedure begins, generating a transmission risk map.

2. **Ring Containment with Active Case Finding** — The single-host drydock as a geographic quarantine cordon. No concurrent hosts, no overlapping exposure windows. Subterranean hazmat channels as the case isolation pathway — removing index-case material before secondary transmission. Analogous to pit latrines in cholera field response: intercept the fecal-oral route before it completes.

3. **Non-Contact Case Management** — Land drones eliminate the index contact (the unprotected health worker inside the exposure radius). Automated cranes are mechanical barriers maintaining pathogen compartmentalization during highest-risk procedures — equivalent to laminar flow hoods during BSL-3 specimen handling. CLASP is the incident command structure.

**Structural Principle:** *Exposure pathway interdiction before community entry, not post-exposure harm reduction. Characterize the reservoir, isolate it geographically, mechanically intercept release events, and remove the susceptible human host from the transmission chain entirely. Move the intervention upstream to the pre-transmission window.*

### Theater / Stage Management

**Reframe:** The ship is a massive, toxic set in a touring production that has been running for decades in community theaters worldwide — houses with inadequate fly systems, no wing space, undertrained crews, and no union oversight. Crew injuries are frequent. The production keeps getting licensed because the script (scrap metal) has value.

**Solution:** Build a dedicated black box facility purpose-built for striking this production. Run the strike as a **fully cued production**, not a demolition.

- **Pre-production (recon drones):** Camera operators survey every lineset, every practical, every piece of rigging. The SM builds the cue stack from this survey. Nothing is touched until the cue sheet is complete.
- **The fly floor (automated cranes):** Every vertical element has a designated lineset and fly cue. When separated from the grid, it stays flown until its receiving position is confirmed clear. The cranes are the fly crew — they wait for their call.
- **The deck crew (logistics drones):** Ground crew moves on **running tracks** — fixed paths that do not cross during live cues. No improvisation, no crossing the center line during a live fly.
- **The trap system (subterranean hazmat):** Professional venues with practicals design drainage into the deck before the show loads in. The hazmat doesn't flow into the house because the stage is built to contain it.

**Structural Principle:** *Safety and efficiency in complex, high-consequence disassembly come from converting a demolition into a scored performance. A score assigns every action a position in time, a responsible party, and a confirmation before release. It eliminates simultaneous freelance decisions — which is where every injury originates. If you can write the score, you can run the show.*

### Ecology / Ecosystem Dynamics

**Reframe:** The ship is a whale fall — a massive, chemically complex organism that has died in an environment not evolved to process it. The pathological status quo is **uncontrolled succession** — opportunistic pioneer scavengers performing chaotic primary breakdown with no management of chemical byproducts, high trophic-level casualties, and toxic legacy in the substrate.

**Solution:** **Mesocosm decomposition** — isolate the detrital input in a bounded experimental unit (the drydock as mesocosm), then deploy a **hierarchical detritivore guild** in strict succession order:

- CLASP as the **keystone regulator** — a mycorrhizal network signaling resource availability and queuing decomposer activity
- Aerial recon drones as the **sensory tier** (chemoreceptor analogues) — continuously mapping chemical gradients and structural integrity
- Ground drones as the **mobile detritivore tier** — performing mechanical shredding and transport (like isopods and amphipods in marine sediment)
- Automated cranes providing **snag management** — maintaining structural integrity of standing dead material long enough for guild succession to complete (premature collapse disperses the substrate chaotically)
- Subterranean hazmat as the **leachate capture system** — impermeable liners routing lipid-phase recalcitrant compounds to holding pools
- Single-ship processing as **monoculture decomposition batching** — preventing cross-contamination between detrital inputs of different chemical profiles

**Structural Principle:** *Decomposition fails when the detritivore guild is mismatched to the detrital input's chemical complexity, and when the substrate has no leachate barrier. Close the system, sequence the guild, capture the recalcitrant fraction. The coordination network is not the innovation — the mesocosm boundary is. Without closed substrate, no coordination system prevents leaching.*

## Synthesis

### Novel Principles (uncommon in the native domain)

**From Epidemiology — Recharacterizing salvage as a transmission chain:** This is the most novel reframe. It treats ship dismantling not as a mechanical problem with safety constraints but as a transmission chain with index cases and exposure windows. The practical payoff: identifying "release events" (breached fuel tanks, fractured hydraulic lines) as discrete index cases justifies pre-event surveillance investment at a level that engineering safety culture would not. The drone recon network stops being a nice-to-have and becomes a sentinel surveillance requirement.

**From Ecology — The mesocosm boundary as the entire intervention:** The closed system boundary is not a feature of the drydock — it IS the entire intervention. Every other element (sequencing, drones, cranes) is downstream of whether the substrate is closed. This is a genuine priority inversion: engineering approaches typically treat containment as a compliance layer added to a process. The ecology framing makes containment the process. The implication for CLASP design is non-trivial: guild succession is adaptive, not scripted — the coordination system may need to respond to what it finds, not just execute a predetermined sequence.

### Convergent Principles (multiple domains arrived independently — high robustness)

**1. Remove the human agent from the high-consequence contact zone (3/4 domains):**
- Cuisine: hook the carcass, use both hands for the knife
- Epidemiology: eliminate the unprotected health worker from the transmission radius
- Theater: deck crew on fixed tracks, no improvisation under live flies

This convergence is strong evidence that the drone logistics network is not incidental — it is the load-bearing element. The case for it should be made on human-removal grounds, not cost or speed.

**2. Single-unit processing as contamination control (4/4 domains):**
- One carcass at a time
- One host in quarantine
- One show per house
- One ship per drydock

All four domains treat single-unit processing as a contamination-control mechanism, not merely an efficiency choice. The sequencing logic only works when inputs do not overlap — cross-contamination between detrital inputs (ecology), overlapping exposure windows (epidemiology), and simultaneous lineset moves (theater) all describe the same failure mode.

**3. Single coordinating intelligence that does not do work (3/4 domains):**
- Cuisine: the expeditor calls tickets but does not cook
- Theater: the SM calls cues but does not strike
- Epidemiology: incident command allocates but does not treat

CLASP should orchestrate, not execute. This separation is load-bearing.

### Reframing Insights

**Epidemiology dissolves the engineering frame entirely.** The problem stops being "how do we dismantle ships more safely" and becomes "how do we interrupt a transmission chain before community exposure is established." This changes what counts as success, what interventions are justified, and where in the process you invest.

**Ecology reframes the drydock from a workspace into a mesocosm.** The containment boundary becomes the central design requirement rather than a cost center. The sequencing logic should be modeled on guild succession (adaptive, responsive to what is found) rather than industrial process flow (predetermined sequence). CLASP may need to be more adaptive than scripted.
